A.P.
1948 — 2025

Architect · Educator · Humanist

Prof. [Full Name]

Faculty of Architecture — [University Name]

Professor [Name] devoted more than four decades to the discipline of architecture not merely as the science of building, but as an act of listening — to place, to community, to the slow sediment of culture that accumulates in walls, thresholds, and courtyards. He believed that a building was a conversation across generations, and he taught his students to enter that conversation with humility.

Born in [City] in 1948, he completed his undergraduate studies at the [University] before pursuing graduate work in [Country], where he encountered the writings of Kenneth Frampton and the emerging discourse on Critical Regionalism. He returned home not with imported answers, but with sharper questions about what an architecture rooted in local materials, climate, and social memory might look like.

His built work — modest in scale, radical in care — spans community libraries, river-edge housing, and a handful of civic buildings that have outlasted their original briefs because they were designed to be altered, to be claimed, to be lived in fully. His scholarship, particularly his landmark Dwelling in the Margin (1997), established him as one of Southeast Asia's most searching voices on the ethics of place.

He is survived by his wife [Name], his children [Name] and [Name], and by the hundreds of students who carry his questions into their own practices. This site is a space for all who were shaped by his presence to leave a trace of that encounter.

A Life in Architecture

Timeline of Work & Thought

Career milestones, published ideas, and built works — arranged as one might read an architectural monograph.

1970

Undergraduate Degree

Graduated with Honours from the Faculty of Architecture, [University]. His final thesis — a low-cost river-edge housing proposal — already bore the hallmarks of his later thought.

1976

Graduate Studies Abroad

Pursued postgraduate research at [European University], engaging deeply with Kenneth Frampton's emerging Critical Regionalism discourse. Returned with a framework for thinking architecturally about place and resistance.

1982

First Major Built Work

Completed the [Community Library Name], [City] — a compact civic building that drew on local brick-laying traditions while opening its central courtyard to the rhythm of the surrounding kampung.

1990

Appointed Full Professor

Named Chair of Design Theory at [University]. His studio became known as a space where social questions were as rigorously examined as structural ones.

1997 · Publication

Dwelling in the Margin

His most widely read book. An argument that the informal city is not a problem to be solved but a living archive of spatial intelligence — one that formal architecture would do well to apprentice itself to.

2003

UNESCO Heritage Consultancy

Served on the UNESCO advisory panel for the conservation of living heritage districts across Southeast Asia, advocating for resident-centred approaches over tourism-driven restoration.

2015

Lifetime Achievement Award

Received the [National Architecture Association] Lifetime Achievement Award. In his acceptance speech he said: "The only award I truly want is to walk through a neighbourhood and hear children playing in a courtyard I designed."

2024 · Final Lecture

"On Building for the Unborn"

His last public lecture, delivered to a standing audience at [University]. A meditation on intergenerational responsibility in the built environment. The recording is available here.
